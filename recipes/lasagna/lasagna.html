Lasagna is a classic Italian dish featuring layers of wide, flat pasta sheets stacked with rich fillings. Typically, these layers include a savory meat sauce made with ground beef or sausage,
a creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese mixture, and a generous topping of melted mozzarella or Parmesan cheese. Baked until bubbling and golden, lasagna offers a comforting blend of flavors and textures in every bite.

Lasagna often starts with a base of meat sauce, which is slow-cooked with tomatoes, onions, garlic, and herbs, creating a deep, flavorful foundation. The cheese layer, whether it's a smooth
ricotta blend or a béchamel sauce, adds a creamy richness that contrasts beautifully with the savory sauce. As it bakes, the pasta absorbs flavors and becomes tender, while the top layer of cheese forms a golden, crispy crust.
Each serving delivers a satisfying combination of hearty meat, velvety cheese, and tender pasta, making lasagna a beloved comfort food around the world.

Ingredients

  • Meat
  • Onion and Garlic
  • Tomato Products
  • Sugar
  • Spices and Seasonings
  • Lasagna Noodles
  • Cheeses
  • Egg

Steps

  1. Make the meat sauce.
  2. Cook the noodles
  3. Make the ricotta mixture.
  4. Layer the lasagna according to the recipe instructions.
  5. Cover with foil and bake.
  6. Let the lasagna rest before serving.

Layering

  1. Meat Sauce
  2. Noodles
  3. Ricotta Mixture
  4. Mozzarella Slices
  5. Meat Sauce
  6. Parmesan Cheese
  7. Repeat the layers, then top with the remaining Parmesan.

How Long to Cook Lasagna

The assembled lasagna should take about 50 minutes to cook in an oven preheated to 375 degrees F. Cover it with foil for the first 25 minutes, then let it cook uncovered for the final 25 minutes.
Also, it's important to let the lasagna rest at room temperature for about 15 minutes before you cut into it.
