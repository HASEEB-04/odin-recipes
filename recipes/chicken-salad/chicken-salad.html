Chicken salad is a versatile dish made with cooked, diced chicken mixed with various ingredients like crisp vegetables, fruits, nuts, and a creamy dressing or mayonnaise. Often served chilled, it can be enjoyed on its own
in a sandwich, or over a bed of greens. The combination of tender chicken with crunchy or sweet elements creates a refreshing and satisfying meal.

Chicken salad typically starts with tender, cooked chicken—whether poached, grilled, or roasted—cut into bite-sized pieces. This is combined with ingredients such as celery, apples, grapes, or nuts, adding a range of textures and flavors.
The mixture is then bound together with a dressing, usually made from mayonnaise, yogurt, or a vinaigrette, providing creaminess and tang. Often seasoned with herbs and spices,
chicken salad can be customized with additional ingredients like avocado or cheese.

How long does homemade chicken salad last?

Homemade chicken salad lasts about 4 days in an airtight container in the refrigerator.
